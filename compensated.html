Compensated Foundation

Compensated foundation works on the principle that if the load of the excavated material is equal to the weight of the building added then no additional stresses is applied on the soil. It is also called as floating foundation. Compensated foundation consists of deep basements which are used to support high rise buildings and swimming pools, in which large amount of material is excavated. It is a very convenient method of construction as it has minimal impact on adjacent structure.


Advantages of Compensated Foundation

  • Simple to construct with minimal impact and disturbance to neighbors.
  • Consolidation settlement is eliminated or controlled.
  • It provides protection to rising damp, drainage and thermal insulation

Disadvantages of Compensated Foundation

  • Comparatively primitive technology
  • Lesser options for design
  • No underground access for utility lines
